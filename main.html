Power spectral density sanity test

Computing power spectral denisty (PSD) with the FFT is easily messed up. A simple check is to add a random signal that is uniformly distributed from $-\Delta/2$ to $\Delta/2$, and compare with the well known theoretical PSD of $\Delta^2/12/f_s$ ($f_s$ is the sample rate).
This signal is often used to model the quantization noise from an ideal [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analog-to-digital_converter analog-to-digital converter] (ADC) and [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital-to-analog_converter digital to analog converter] (DAC) (with step $\Delta$).
